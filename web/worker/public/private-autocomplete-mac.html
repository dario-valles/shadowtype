Built for privacy first

A typing assistant that doesn’t phone home

Most AI autocomplete tools route your keystrokes to a server to predict the next word. Shadowtype doesn’t — the model runs on-device as a local LLM and works fully offline. For privacy-conscious users, legal and compliance-sensitive workplaces, and anyone who doesn’t want a cloud keyboard reading their typing, that’s the whole point. Read our full privacy policy.

Keystrokes never leave the Mac

Inference happens locally on Apple Silicon. There is no completion server, so your keystrokes, drafts, and predictions are never uploaded, logged, or shared. Turn Wi-Fi off and it keeps working.

No telemetry, no account

Shadowtype ships with zero analytics backend — nothing to opt out of, because nothing is collected. You never sign up or log in. The app just runs locally, the moment you launch it.

Password fields are never read

Secure and password input fields are ignored entirely. macOS flags them as sensitive, and Shadowtype generates no ghost text and captures nothing there — your credentials are off-limits by design.

Personalization stays encrypted on-device

The optional style profile that learns your tone is stored locally and encrypted on your Mac — never uploaded. Wipe it any time with one click and start fresh, no questions asked.

You keep your copyright

Because nothing you write is sent anywhere, there is no clause handing your text to a vendor for training. What you draft is yours — full copyright, full ownership, no fine print.

Works in every app, privately

Continuous inline ghost text in any text field — mail, docs, chat, code. Tab accepts a word, ⌥Tab a whole line, and ⌥⌘K rewrites a selection — all on-device.

Local API + MCP for developers

An OpenAI-compatible HTTP endpoint runs on 127.0.0.1 so your scripts, editors, and agents can call the same on-device model — no key, no cloud. A built-in MCP bridge plugs into Claude Code and other MCP clients, and the BYOM picker loads any GGUF.

How privacy is enforced

Local by architecture, not by promise

Privacy here isn’t a setting you trust — it’s how the app is built. There’s simply no path for your text to leave the machine.

1. The model lives on your Mac

You pick a GGUF model from the catalog (Qwen3 Base, Gemma 3, MoE variants — or bring your own) and it runs entirely on Apple Silicon. Predictions are computed locally — there is no API call to a completion server, ever. The optional developer-facing API binds to 127.0.0.1 only.

2. Sensitive fields are skipped

Password and secure input fields are never read. The app respects the system’s secure-field flag and produces no suggestions where you’d type a credential.

3. Your style stays sealed on-device

Any personalization is stored encrypted on your Mac and never uploaded. One click wipes it. With zero telemetry and no account, there’s nothing about you on any server.

Questions

Private autocomplete FAQ

How private is it really?
Completely. Shadowtype runs the language model entirely on your Mac. Your keystrokes, drafts, and predictions never leave the device — there’s no completion server to send them to. It works with Wi-Fi off, nothing you type is uploaded or logged, and you keep full copyright of everything you write.
Does it have telemetry or an account?
No. Shadowtype ships with zero telemetry and no analytics backend — there’s nothing to opt out of, because nothing is collected or sent. You don’t create an account or log in; the app just runs locally on your Mac.
Are password fields read?
No. Shadowtype never reads secure or password input fields. macOS marks those fields as secure and the app ignores them entirely — no ghost text is generated there and nothing is captured. The same applies to any field the system flags as sensitive.
Where is my personalization data stored?
On your Mac, encrypted. The optional style profile that learns your tone is stored locally and encrypted on-device — it’s never uploaded. You can wipe it at any time with one click, and the app keeps working with a fresh profile.
Can my own scripts, editors, and agents use the same local model?
Yes. Shadowtype exposes an OpenAI-compatible HTTP endpoint on 127.0.0.1 with no key required, so your terminal, editor plugins, and AI agents can call /v1/chat/completions against the same on-device model. A built-in MCP server plugs into Claude Code and other MCP clients, and the BYOM picker loads any GGUF you drop in. Everything is bound to localhost — nothing leaves your Mac.
